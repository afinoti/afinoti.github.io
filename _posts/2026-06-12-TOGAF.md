## What is it?

TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework) is an enterprise architecture framework developed and maintained by **The Open Group**. It provides a comprehensive methodology and set of tools for developing, planning, implementing, and governing enterprise IT architectures.

It is one of the most widely adopted frameworks for aligning IT strategy with business goals.

---

## The Four Architecture Domains

TOGAF organizes architecture into four interconnected domains:

| Domain | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Business** | Business processes, functions, goals, and organizational structure |
| **Data / Information** | Logical and physical data assets and data management resources |
| **Application** | Individual application systems and their interactions |
| **Technology** | Hardware, software, and network infrastructure |

---

## ADM — Architecture Development Method

The ADM is the core of TOGAF. It is a cyclic, iterative process that guides architects through the development of an enterprise architecture.

### Phases

- **Preliminary** — Prepare the organization; define principles and tailoring of the framework
- **A: Architecture Vision** — Define scope, stakeholders, and high-level vision
- **B: Business Architecture** — Develop the baseline and target business architecture
- **C: Information Systems Architecture** — Data and application architectures
- **D: Technology Architecture** — Technology infrastructure to support the above
- **E: Opportunities & Solutions** — Identify roadmap and transition architectures
- **F: Migration Planning** — Prioritize and plan migration projects
- **G: Implementation Governance** — Oversee implementation of the architecture
- **H: Architecture Change Management** — Manage changes to the architecture
- **Requirements Management** — Central phase that feeds and receives input from all phases

## Key Concepts

### Architecture Repository
A structured store for all architecture-related outputs, including:
- Architecture Metamodel
- Architecture Capability
- Architecture Landscape
- Reference Library
- Standards Information Base

### Enterprise Continuum
A classification model for reusing architecture assets ranging from generic (Foundation Architecture) to organization-specific (Organization-Specific Architecture).

### Architecture Capability Framework
Defines the organizational structures, roles, responsibilities, and skills needed to operate an effective enterprise architecture practice.

---

## Why use TOGAF?

- Provides a **common language** for architects and stakeholders
- Ensures **alignment between business and IT**
- Reduces risk through **structured governance**
- Enables **reuse** of architecture components across the organization
- Supports **compliance** with standards and regulatory requirements

---

## TOGAF vs Other Frameworks

| Framework | Focus | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **TOGAF** | Holistic EA methodology | Large enterprises, IT-business alignment |
| **Zachman** | Classification taxonomy | Cataloging and organizing architecture artifacts |
| **SAFe** | Agile at scale | Software delivery and agile transformation |
| **ITIL** | IT service management | Operations and service delivery |

---

## Certifications

The Open Group offers two levels of TOGAF certification:

- **TOGAF Foundation (Level 1)** — Basic understanding of the framework
- **TOGAF Certified (Level 2)** — Ability to analyze and apply TOGAF in practice

The current version is **TOGAF Standard, Version 10** (released 2022).
